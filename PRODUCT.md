# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Federal government contracting officers, program managers, and IT procurement leads — primarily at the VA and similar civilian agencies — evaluating IT services vendors. They arrive via referral or a capability statement and need to form trust in minutes, not hours. Secondary: veteran job seekers looking for a company that gets them.

## Product Purpose

KJB Solutions is a veteran-owned small business delivering program management, software development, and infrastructure consulting to the federal government. The site's job is to signal immediate credibility, surface the three service lines clearly, make the veteran-owned identity a differentiator (not a footnote), and drive contact/inquiry. Success = a contracting officer reading the page and feeling confident enough to reach out.

## Brand Personality

Authoritative · Mission-driven · Modern. Military discipline in the structure; forward-leaning confidence in the execution; veteran pride woven into the narrative — not worn on the sleeve. The brand sounds like a senior officer who's also a technologist: precise, direct, no fluff.

## Anti-references

- Generic gov-contractor palette: muted navy/gray/flag stock photos that every mid-tier contractor uses.
- WordPress theme feel: the current site — low hierarchy, template-shaped, dated.
- SaaS startup minimalism: Stripe-white hero-metric aesthetic with no imagery and thin type.
- Heavy military cliché: bald eagle imagery, "INTEGRITY" in 80px caps, stock handshake overuse.

## Design Principles

1. **Discipline as design language.** Structure, grid, and hierarchy should feel deliberate — the layout itself communicates reliability.
2. **Mission first.** Every section earns its place by answering "why does a contracting officer care?" Remove or demote anything that doesn't serve that question.
3. **Veteran identity as differentiator, not decoration.** The story is that KJB uses the software they build. That insider understanding should be felt in the copy and composition, not just stated.
4. **Modern federal, not generic federal.** Dark, committed navy surfaces — not beige neutrals. Color strategy is Committed (navy carries 60%+ of the surface).
5. **Dynamic but purposeful.** Scroll-driven reveals, hero motion, and transitions should feel earned and measured — not decorative.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA minimum. Respect prefers-reduced-motion. All images need meaningful alt text. Keyboard navigation required throughout.
